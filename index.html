01 — The premise

Your standard health checkup is built to find disease, not to optimize health.

The 12-marker corporate panel will catch you when you're already in trouble. It won't tell you that your insulin is creeping, your inflammation is sub-clinical, or that your apolipoprotein-B is laying down arterial plaque while your LDL "looks normal."

PROBLEM I.

You can't optimize what you don't measure.

Most adults under 45 have no idea what their fasting insulin, HbA1c, ApoB, or hsCRP actually are — let alone whether they've moved over the last twelve months. You're navigating in the dark.

PROBLEM II.

Generic advice doesn't work because you're not generic.

Your liver, your gut microbiome, your genetics, your training history, and your stress profile are not the same as the influencer telling you to do cold plunges. A protocol calibrated to you works. One borrowed from someone else mostly doesn't.

PROBLEM III.

"Wellness" has no accountability.

You buy supplements, hire a trainer, try intermittent fasting, sleep eight hours — and at the end of the year, you have no idea what worked. The feedback loop is missing. Without it, you're guessing forever.

PROBLEM IV.

The good doctors don't have time to be coaches.

Your physician has eleven minutes. They're not going to redesign your eating window, calibrate your zone-2 cardio, or tell you which three of your sixteen supplements are actually pulling weight. That's not what they're there for.

07 — Questions

The things we get asked, honestly answered.

If your question isn't here, we're an email away. We'd rather have a longer conversation before you commit than have you sign up unsure.

How is this different from a corporate health checkup or a Cult.fit program?
A corporate health checkup runs 12–15 markers, generates a PDF, and is forgotten in two weeks. A fitness program prescribes generic workouts. We do neither. We run 50+ deep markers (most of which corporate panels skip — ApoB, Lp(a), fasting insulin, hsCRP), build a personalized intervention from your specific data, support you through 90 days of behavioural change, and then re-measure. The before-and-after is the entire point.
Are you a clinic? Do you have doctors?
We are a coaching and analytics platform with a medical advisor. We work with hospital-grade laboratories for bloodwork (NABL accredited) and our protocols are reviewed by a longevity-focused physician. We are not your primary care provider. We work alongside your doctor, not in place of them.
Does this work for women in their 30s and 40s?
Yes — and the panel includes a complete sex-hormone profile and considers cycle timing for bloodwork. The intervention adjusts for menstrual phase, perimenopause considerations, and hormone-related metabolic shifts. We don't apply male-default protocols to women. Different physiology, different prescription.
What if my biomarkers don't improve in 90 days?
We re-test, regardless of the outcome. If a marker hasn't moved, the protocol adapts — and we extend the engagement at no extra cost until that marker has been addressed properly. Our reputation depends on outcomes, so we treat non-movement as our problem, not yours. We'll tell you transparently which markers are slow movers (some take 6+ months, biologically) and which aren't budging because of compliance vs. biology.
Where do I get the bloodwork done?
A licensed phlebotomist visits your home or office for the fasted blood draw — no clinic visit required. Samples go directly to our partner NABL-accredited lab. Typical turnaround for full results is 5–7 working days. Available across Hyderabad, with expansion to Bangalore and Pune in the next quarter.
What happens to my data?
Your data is yours. You own it, you can export it, you can delete it. We use anonymized, aggregated data to improve our protocols across cohorts — never identifiable individual data. We never sell data, period. We're not an ad-supported platform and never will be. Our data ethics policy is published in full at /privacy.
Can I do this if I'm already on medication?
In most cases, yes — and our medical advisor will review your current medications before designing your protocol. We never recommend stopping or changing prescription medication. For some active conditions, we may suggest waiting until you've stabilized with your physician first. The free initial consultation is partly to figure this out.
